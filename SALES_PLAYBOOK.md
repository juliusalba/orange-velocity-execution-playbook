## Phase 2: Discovery Call (Days 4-7)

### Pre-Call Preparation

**Research Checklist:**
- [ ] Visit company website
- [ ] Check LinkedIn profile
- [ ] Look at their current ads (if visible)
- [ ] Check their Facebook page
- [ ] Review their Google Ads (if running)
- [ ] Note key metrics (followers, engagement, etc.)

**Prepare Materials:**
- [ ] Relevant case study (printed or PDF)
- [ ] Your service packages (printed)
- [ ] Calculator for ROI projections
- [ ] Calendar to book next meeting

### Discovery Call Structure (30 minutes)

**1. Opening & Agenda (2 minutes):**
```
"Thanks for taking the time to speak with me, [First Name].
Before we dive in, I want to make sure this call is valuable for you.

My goal today is to understand your business, identify your biggest challenges, and see if we're a good fit to work together.

If it makes sense, I'll share some insights from our work with similar e-commerce brands, and we can explore whether a partnership could help you reach your goals.

How does that sound?"
```

**2. Business Understanding (8 minutes):**
```
"Great! Let's start with some basics about your business.

1. Tell me about [Company] - what do you sell and to whom?
2. How did you get into the [industry] space?
3. What does success look like for your business in the next 6-12 months?
4. What does your current marketing mix look like?
5. How long have you been running ads?
6. What's your current monthly ad spend across all platforms?

[TAKE NOTES ON ALL ANSWERS]"
```

**3. Pain Point Identification (10 minutes):**
```
"Thanks for sharing that. Now let's talk about challenges.

1. What prompted you to look for help with your ads right now?
2. What's your biggest challenge with your current advertising?
3. How is your CAC trending over the past 3-6 months?
4. What's your current conversion rate from ad click to purchase?
5. What would doubling your ROAS mean for your business?
6. Have you worked with a paid ads agency before? What worked/didn't work?

[LISTEN FOR SPECIFIC PAINS - DOCUMENT THEM]"
```

**4. Budget & Decision Process (7 minutes):**
```
"That makes sense. Now let's talk about working together.

1. What would you need to see to feel confident about investing in an agency?
2. What's your budget for agency fees per month?
3. Who else is involved in this decision?
4. What's your timeline for making a decision?
5. If we could reduce your CAC by 30% within 60 days, what would that be worth to your business?
6. Are you looking to make a decision before or after [upcoming event - Q4, funding, etc.]?

[CONFIRM BUDGET, TIMELINE, DECISION-MAKERS]"
```

**5. Value Positioning & Next Steps (3 minutes):**
```
"Based on what you've shared, I think we could definitely help you reach your goals. We've worked with similar brands in [industry] and have a proven system for reducing CAC and scaling ads.

Here's what I'd recommend as next steps:

1. Schedule a strategy session where we'll do a deep dive audit of your current ads
2. Create a custom 90-day growth plan for [Company]
3. Present our findings and recommendations
4. If it makes sense, we'll discuss how to move forward

How does that sound?

[IF YES]: Book strategy session
[IF HESITANT]: What concerns do you have?
[IF NO]: No problem at all. When would make more sense?"
```

### Discovery Call Objection Handling

# SALES PLAYBOOK: ICP #3 - Traditional Business Going Digital

## Key Differences

**Longer Sales Cycle (30-45 days)**
**Education Needed**
**Price Sensitive**
**Need Hand-Holding**

## Outreach Strategy

### Email Template
```
Subject: Help for [Industry] businesses going digital

Hi [First Name],

I noticed [Company] has been serving [city/region] for [X] years.

With more customers moving online, I'm helping traditional [industry] businesses expand into digital channels.

Recently helped a [similar business] go from zero online presence to generating PHP 200k/month in online sales.

Would you be interested in learning how they did it?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Discovery Call Approach

**Be More Educational:**
- Explain digital marketing basics
- Show examples of similar businesses succeeding
- Emphasize the "why now" (market shift)
- Offer free resources (guides, checklists)

**Pain Points:**
- "We're behind the times"
- "We don't understand technology"
- "We're afraid of wasting money"
- "Our customers are older (don't use internet)"

**Solution Positioning:**
- Position as "digital transformation partner"
- Offer training/education as part of service
- Show testimonials from similar businesses
- Offer guarantees to reduce risk

## Objection Handling Mastery

### Objection: "We tried an agency before and it didn't work"
**Response:**
```
"I understand - and that's actually why we do things differently. Most agencies take a one-size-fits-all approach.

We spend the first 30 days doing a deep audit and creating a custom strategy for YOUR business. Then we test systematically and iterate based on data.

Can I ask - what specifically didn't work with the previous agency?"
```

### Objection: "Your price is too high"
**Response:**
```
"I appreciate you being upfront about that. Help me understand - when you say it's too high, are you comparing it to other agencies, or is it just not in your budget right now?

[IF BUDGET]: What budget were you working with?
[IF COMPARISON]: Here's what you get for that investment: [show deliverables + ROI]

You know, price is what you pay, value is what you get. If we can reduce your CAC by 30%, what would that be worth to your business?"
```


## Final Tips

### Top 5 Success Factors
1. **Focus on ICP #1 (E-commerce)** - highest conversion rate
2. **Personalize every message** - no templates without customization
3. **Lead with value, not features** - what can you do for them?
4. **Ask for the sale** - assumptive closing works
5. **Follow up relentlessly** - 80% of sales happen after the 5th follow-up

### Biggest Mistakes to Avoid
1. **Talking too much** - listen 70%, talk 30%
2. **Focusing on price** - focus on ROI and value
3. **Weak follow-up** - most deals are lost here
4. **Not qualifying properly** - pursue the right leads
5. **No urgency** - create urgency in every conversation
